# Club BAIST – Business Problem

Club BAIST is an exclusive private golf club, founded in 1996. The 18-hole golf course is an award-winning and extremely challenging design that can be enjoyed by golfers at all levels. The golf club’s board of directors has decided that a few areas of their business operation would benefit from computerized automation. This includes tee times, memberships, and player scores.

## Tee Times

Due to the Alberta climate, golf at the club course can be played regularly from the beginning of April to the end of September. Depending on the current year’s weather conditions, there is the possibility of starting earlier or finishing later in the year. As a result, booking reservations for tee times to play golf are at a premium.

All members in good standing are allowed to book reservations for golf tee times. Tee time reservations are currently taken over the phone or in person and are entered onto a daily tee sheet.

The golf club accepts reservations for **1 to 4 golfers**. The club schedules each grouping of up to 4 golfers at approximately **8-minute intervals** in order to accommodate a manageable number of golfers on the course at one time.

Tee times can be made up to **one week in advance** and, as a result, there may be alterations required in order to accommodate changing member schedules. If any tee time cannot be met, it is the responsibility of the member to contact the golf club and cancel. Otherwise, the member will be reprimanded appropriately.

### Standing Tee Time Requests

Shareholder members can make **one standing tee time request** that is posted each week, and it must be for a **foursome only**. Members with this extra privilege fill out a standing tee time request card indicating the desired tee time. The golf club’s rules committee assigns a priority to each request in case of conflicts.

#### Standing Tee Time Request – Fields

- List Shareholder Member first
- Member Number / Name (4 entries)
- Requested Day of Week
- Requested Tee Time (± 30 min)
- Requested Start Date
- Requested End Date

**Staff-only / shaded area:**

- Priority Number
- Approved Tee Time
- Approved By
- Approved Date

A tee sheet for each golf day is created **one week in advance**. Special events, such as tournaments, may restrict the number of available tee times. As a result, a clerk fills in any special events and crosses off any affected tee times. The clerk then goes through the standing tee time request cards and schedules the appropriate tee times on the tee sheet. The clerk then schedules all phone requests on a **first call, first served** basis.

On the golf day, the tee sheet is transferred to the pro shop where it is used by the club’s golf professional and the pro shop staff to manage and coordinate golf course use. At this point, any modifications to the daily golf schedule are managed by the pro shop staff. The pro shop staff is responsible for accommodating any phone and in-person golf-day requests. All players must check in with the pro shop staff prior to playing.

## Memberships

The club currently has a few levels of membership. The membership committee is considering the possibility of expanding these.

### Membership Levels and Fees

#### Gold

- Shareholder — **$3,000.00**
- Associate — **$4,500.00**

#### Silver

- Shareholder Spouse — **$2,000.00**
- Associate Spouse — **$2,500.00**

#### Bronze

- Pee Wee (dependent, age 6–11) — **$250.00**
- Junior (dependent, age 12–17) — **$500.00**
- Intermediate (dependent, age 18–24) — **$1,000.00**

#### Copper

- Social (no golf privileges) — **$100.00**

### Additional Fees and Billing Rules

- Golf club share purchase price: **$1,000.00**
- Entrance fee: **$10,000.00**
- Entrance fee may be paid over **two years** in **4 equal payments of $2,500.00**
- All yearly membership fees are due **April 1** of the current year
- A **10% penalty** is applied to overdue balances
- All fees **do not include GST**

**Additional rule:** Gold memberships marked with `*` have an additional minimum **food and beverage charge of $500.00 yearly**.

### Membership Sponsorship and Approval Rules

Any prospective member must be sponsored by **two existing club shareholder members**.

Any shareholder who:

- has been a member for at least **5 years**, and
- is in **good standing**

can sponsor up to **2 prospective members per year**.

Prospective members submit an application for membership to the golf club. The golf club’s membership committee reviews all applications **once per month** to determine suitability for membership.

Applications can be:

- accepted
- denied
- on-hold
- waitlisted

Once an application has been accepted, an account is created and maintained for the new member.

The membership committee would like to keep an automated record of all applications and their status. The finance committee would also like to record and track each member’s account and associated activity at the club.

After completing a round of golf, a member can turn in their scorecard. Scores are processed by a clerk and a resulting report is made available.

### Member Handicap Index Report

**Fields shown:**

- Date
- Member Name
- Handicap Index
- Last 20 Average
- Best 8 Average
- Last 20 Round Scores

Club BAIST wants each member to electronically provide their own scoring, which must follow the guidelines of the **World Handicapping System (WHS)**.

### Required Data for Each Golf Round

- Date
- Golf Course
- Course Rating
- Slope Rating
- Hole-by-Hole Score
- Total Score

If members play a different golf course, they can also incorporate that scoring as long as the golf course is approved by **Golf Canada**.

> “Golf Canada is the Authorized National body that is responsible for implementing and administering the Rules of Handicapping in Canada in co-operation with the provincial golf associations.”

> “The purpose of the World Handicap System (WHS) is to make the game of golf more enjoyable for golfers by providing a consistent means of measuring one’s performance and progress and to enable golfers of differing abilities to compete, or play a casual round, with anyone else on a fair and equal basis.”
